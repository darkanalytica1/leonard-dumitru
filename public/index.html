What I Actually Do – Four Pillars

Systems & Architecture Mapping

I audit and map existing tools, microservices, code repositories, flows and responsibilities.

Then define improved architecture and ownership for each part of the system.

  • Audit stacks across APIs, CI/CD, BI, infrastructure, workflows and team responsibilities.
  • Identify tech debt, blind spots, missing monitoring and areas where nobody is truly accountable.
  • Turn chaos into phased roadmaps with realistic milestones, KPIs and accountable owners instead of vague “improvements”.
Product & Revenue Innovation

I analyse existing assets and design new, market-fit products or business lines around them.

  • Turn byproduct data and assets into additional, structured revenue streams rather than leaving them as noise in the stack.
  • Translate technical capabilities into commercially viable GTM paths that sales and partners can actually explain and sell.
  • Identify niches where existing resources solve high-value problems without requiring a full rebuild of the platform.
Operational Intelligence & Clarity

I convert fragmented, undocumented systems into understandable frameworks for planning, prioritisation and delivery.

  • Map workflows, responsibilities and data paths across teams and tools.
  • Replace reactive decision-making with predictable, documented behaviour and decision criteria.
  • Ensure cross-functional teams operate with shared definitions of KPIs, ownership and acceptable behaviour.
Stakeholder-Oriented Product Ownership

I work across business, technical stakeholders and leadership so that decisions are correct, documented and aligned with both constraints and market needs.

  • Clarify requirements, remove ambiguity and document decision logic before work begins.
  • Align engineering, data, product and revenue teams around the same architecture and objectives.
  • Ensure operational models reflect strategy rather than growing as uncontrolled side-effects of past decisions.
